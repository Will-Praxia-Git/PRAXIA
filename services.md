## Microsoft Ads for B2B

Microsoft Advertising is the channel most agencies treat as a Google copy-paste afterthought. For B2B it is frequently the better-converting, lower-CPC channel, and it rewards anyone willing to actually build it properly rather than import a campaign and walk away.

This is a core specialism, not a bolt-on.

**What this covers:**

- Full account builds structured for B2B intent, not consumer search patterns
- LinkedIn profile targeting (job function, industry, company) layered onto search and audience campaigns, which is unique to Microsoft and genuinely powerful for B2B
- Import-and-rebuild: taking a sloppy Google import and restructuring it for how Microsoft search behaves, including match type and bid strategy differences
- Microsoft Audience Network campaigns for B2B prospecting, with placement and brand-safety controls
- Custom conversion goals and proper offline conversion import so the platform optimises toward qualified leads and revenue, not raw form fills
- UET tag implementation and debugging, enhanced conversions for leads, and conversion value rules
- Bid strategy migration (Manual/eCPC to tCPA/tROAS) once the conversion data is clean enough to support it
- Negative keyword and search term hygiene tuned for lead quality over volume

If you run B2B and your Microsoft account is "just the Google campaigns imported," that is almost always money left on the table. This is the single highest-leverage area I work in.

## Tracking, Measurement & Custom Conversions

This is the foundation everything else sits on, and it is where most accounts are quietly broken. If the conversion data is wrong, the bidding is wrong, the reporting is wrong, and every optimisation decision after that is built on sand.

**Custom conversions and conversion modelling:**

- Custom conversion definitions in Google Ads and Microsoft Ads that track the actions that actually matter (qualified leads, booked calls, MQLs, revenue) rather than every button click
- Conversion value rules and value-based bidding setup so the platforms optimise toward your most valuable customers
- Primary vs secondary conversion configuration so smart bidding optimises toward the right signal
- Lead-stage conversions imported back from the CRM so the platform learns what a good lead looks like, not just what a lead looks like

**Offline conversion tracking and pipelines:**

- GCLID and MSCLKID capture on the site and into your CRM or spreadsheet
- Offline conversion import pipelines for both Google and Microsoft, so closed-won revenue flows back to the campaign and keyword that generated it
- Enhanced conversions for leads (hashed first-party data) to recover conversions lost to consent and cross-device gaps
- The full loop: click to lead to CRM to closed deal to platform, so bidding optimises on money, not form submissions

**GA4 and GTM implementation:**

- GA4 setup and configuration done properly, including event and conversion design, not just the default install
- Google Tag Manager implementation: container builds, dataLayer design, event tracking, cross-domain tracking
- Tag, trigger and variable architecture that is documented and maintainable rather than a tangle nobody can touch
- Consent Mode v2 implementation
- Server-side tagging where it makes sense
- Linking everything up: GA4 to Google Ads, Merchant Center, Search Console, and the Microsoft equivalents

If you have ever looked at three different numbers for "conversions" across three tools and not known which to trust, this is the work that fixes it.
